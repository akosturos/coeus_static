What We Do

COEUS and its strategic partners with over a century of experience, have intelligently developed, sustainable farming practices, multi-diverse mechanization, and viable industrialization techniques, resulting in unparalleled economic potential. Sustainability and environmental stewardship are core to the culture of COEUS, and its intelligent technology solutions.

Together with our partners, we have successfully integrated sustainable bio-pellet solutions that achieve water, food and energy security, while protecting the ecosystems where it is grown and deployed. Incorporating our bio-pellets for power generation reduces both particulate and greenhouse gas emissions, reducing 13,800 barrels of oil for each 8,300 MT of bio-pellets generating one MW of electricity. Bio-pellets, also offers regions of the world, that are not logistically suitable in producing bioenergy crops, a viable alternative solution to meet their energy needs.

Firm Renewable Power Production

Firm Renewable Power Production

COEUS has developed a sustainable, integrated renewable energy closed-circle solution, that is economically viable for specific regions of the US and its territories. These strategic locales, are typically petroleum reliant and subject to market volatility that provides energy instability.

There are thousands of economically successful dedicated firm biomass combustion facilities around the world spanning the entire range of combustion configurations, feedstocks sources (single dedicated feedstock, to multi-fuel), and scale (individual domestic to large commercial facilities powering millions of homes).

Solid Biofuel

Solid Biofuel

COEUS has developed a vertically integrated solid biofuel solution that is both sustainable and economically competitive. Pelletization allows for a large distribution radius to service co-fired biomass customers, where it is not possible to achieve a scalable production level of biomass. This will enable restrictive regions of the US and its territories to achieve energy security by displacing 13,800 barrels of oil/yr., for every MW of power that a locality may demand.

We have identified the Asian and European marketplace as our target regions, for immediate and decades of solid biofuel growth. Many European facilities are planned for biomass conversions from coal & diesel. The Pacific Rim region is projected to grow exponentially due to strict governmental regulations, limiting nuclear and coal power production from the Asian Pacific region. Two bio-combustion facilities are set to grow by 400% to 1M tons within two years. Another facility plans to increase its use of wood-based fuel, from around 250,000 tons a year in 2016, to 1.2 million tons within two years.

Grain

Grain

COEUS together with its strategic partners can produce a dual-purpose feedstock that can provide energy and food security, enabling economically viable expansion to the livestock, poultry and swine industries. Local feed costs are prohibitive to operate these industries, due to the increased logistical expenses. Biodiverse crops and pelletizing when applicable allow for these markets to achieve self- reliance and stabilize existing operations, where otherwise they could not.
